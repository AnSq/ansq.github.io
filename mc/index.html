I bought Minecraft I think the day after version 1.0 was released, or about 19 November 2011. Sometime around July 2012 I discoved the nerd.nu servers and decided to try them out. At the time, they were running three servers, Creative, PvE (player versus environment, or survival with no PvP), and Survival (with PvP). I don't especially like PvP, and I generally find creative mode boring, so I decided PvE would be best for me.

Nerd.nu servers have “revisions” (or “revs”) that usually last between three and six months. Between revs, the map is replaced and inventories are reset, so each rev is it's own Minecraft world.

Here you can see some of the stuff I've made, my thinking behind some of the ideas, and how I designed some of them, both online and in single player.

The fancier looking pictures at the beginning of each section were rendered in chunky, and the others are in-game screenshots (usually cropped) or scans from my design notebook. Clicking any image will open a full-resolution version in a new tab.
